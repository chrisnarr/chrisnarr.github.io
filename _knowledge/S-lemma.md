- Proposed by Yakubovich ’71

Suppose $\exists \bar{x} \in \mathbb{R}^n$ s.t. $q_a(\bar{x}) > 0$. If

$$
\forall x, \;\; q_a(x) \geq 0 \Rightarrow q_b(x) \geq 0,
$$

then

$$
\exists \lambda \geq 0, \;\; \text{s.t. } \;\;q_b(x) \geq \lambda q_a(x) \;\; \forall x.
$$

- You can think of the second inequality as a **certificate** for the first implication.
