> [!idea] Idea
> The Jacobian matrix of a **vector-valued function** with **multiple** input variables is the matrix of all its **first-order partial derivatives**.

> [!Definition] Definition: Jacobian Matrix
> The Jacobian matrix of a function $f: \mathbb{R}^{n} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^{m}$ is defined as the $m \times n$ matrix
>
> $$
> J =
> \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_1} & \dots & \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_n} \end{bmatrix} =
> \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x_1} & \dots & \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x_n} \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \frac{\partial f_m}{\partial x_1} & \dots & \frac{\partial f_m}{\partial x_n} \end{bmatrix}
> $$
